Scientific Record

Pharmacological Profile

Class Empathogen / Entactogen Phenethylamine Amphetamine
Scientific Name 3,4-Methylenedioxymethamphetamine
Common Names MDMA · Ecstasy · Molly · Mandy · XTC · Pingers · E
Primary Mechanism Monoamine transporter reversal. MDMA enters neurons via monoamine transporters and triggers reverse transport — forcing serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine out of the cell. Unlike reuptake inhibitors, it actively drives release. Serotonin release predominates (~3:1 ratio over dopamine).
Secondary Effects Oxytocin and prolactin release. TAAR1 agonism. Direct 5-HT2A partial agonism. These underlie the prosocial and empathogenic effects distinct from other stimulants.
Half-Life 8 – 9 hours (plasma, oral route)
Onset Oral: 30–60 min · Insufflated: 10–20 min
Duration Oral: 3–5 hours (peak) · After-effects: 2–12 hours
Primary Risks Hyperthermia Hyponatraemia Serotonin Syndrome Cardiovascular Load Press Contamination
Hyperthermia is the leading mechanism of MDMA-related festival fatalities. Hyponatraemia from over-hydration is the second most common. Both are preventable with correct information.
Interactions MAOIs (fatal) Lithium SSRIs / SNRIs Cocaine Tramadol
MAOI combination is potentially lethal via serotonin syndrome. SSRIs both blunt effects and increase serotonin syndrome risk.

Field Intelligence
HARM REDUCTION NOTES ASA-EM-01
Evidence-first · Non-judgemental · Operational
Temperature
MDMA is profoundly thermogenic. The combination of MDMA-driven heat production, physical exertion, and ambient festival temperature creates rapid core temperature elevation. Take a 10-minute break from dancing every hour. Find shade or a cool area. Stopping sweating despite being hot is a red flag requiring immediate assistance.
Hydration
Sip, do not chug. Approximately 500ml per hour when dancing. Electrolyte drinks are preferable to plain water for extended sessions. Hyponatraemia — dangerously low sodium — is caused by over-hydration with plain water. It has caused deaths that were entirely preventable. More water is not always safer.
Dosing
Start low: 75–100mg for an average adult. Avoid re-dosing entirely if possible. A redose does not recreate the first experience — it extends duration and multiplies cardiovascular, thermal, and neurotoxic load. The comedown is worse, and the next-day impact is substantially greater.
Test Your Press
Press contamination with methamphetamine, NBOMe compounds, and fentanyl analogues is documented. Reagent testing (Marquis, Simon's, Mecke) and fentanyl test strips reduce — not eliminate — this risk. Testing services at events provide more reliable analysis than field reagents alone. If a press behaves differently than expected — stop.
Frequency of Use
MDMA has a well-documented neurotoxic potential related to serotonergic depletion with frequent use. A minimum three-month gap between uses is widely cited in harm reduction guidance, not as a moral position but as the approximate time required for serotonin system recovery. Weekly use is high-risk.
Medication Interactions
MAOIs are contraindicated — this combination can be fatal via serotonin syndrome. SSRIs and SNRIs both reduce MDMA effects (leading to higher dosing) and increase serotonin syndrome risk. Tramadol, lithium, and stimulants carry their own interaction profiles. If you take any regular medications, check interactions before use.
Wingmate Protocol
Watch your group. MDMA can mask exhaustion and dehydration — the person most at risk may feel fine. Red flags: person has stopped sweating despite heat, skin is hot and dry, they are confused or unresponsive, they have a rigid jaw or are clenching, they are pale or have a bluish tinge, their temperature feels extreme. Do not wait. Get to the medical or harm reduction team immediately. Tell them what was taken.

Why this plate exists in the archive.

MDMA is the substance that most directly shaped the culture the Altered State Archive was built to serve. Its emergence in rave and festival environments from the late 1980s onward created both the scene and many of its safety challenges. Understanding MDMA specifically — not just stimulants as a class — requires understanding the pharmacology that makes it distinct: the serotonin-dominance of its mechanism, the prosocial effects that arise from oxytocin co-release, and the specific environmental interactions that elevate its risk profile beyond other stimulants.

Public discourse around MDMA is polarised. Medical research into therapeutic applications has generated one frame; moral and legal prohibition has generated another. Neither framing gives festival attendees what they need. The archive's purpose is a third register — precise, evidence-based, and operationally useful in the specific contexts where MDMA is actually encountered: hot, loud, physical environments with limited access to clinical support.

The Triple Monoamine Efflux Model chosen for this plate illustrates the transporter reversal mechanism — the fundamental difference between MDMA and a conventional reuptake inhibitor. The three wave systems represent 5-HT, DA, and NE release in approximate proportion. This model was chosen because it makes the most clinically consequential fact about MDMA visually clear: serotonin is the primary output, and the downstream effects of that — temperature dysregulation, fluid balance disruption, and the interaction profile with serotonergic medications — are the risks that kill people at festivals.
