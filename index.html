The Ecosystem

Three parts.
One system.

Most safety work at festivals happens in silos — a first aid tent over here, a harm reduction stall over there, an incident report filed after the fact. None of it connects. None of it learns.

ViceLab is structured differently. The research arm produces the evidence base. Cooked Pilot delivers it to attendees in real time. VibeGuard gives organisers the visibility to act on it.

Each division is independently useful. Together, they form something that hasn't existed before — an end-to-end harm reduction infrastructure for live events.

The Three Divisions

and why.

Each division has a distinct audience and a specific job. They share a methodology, a values framework, and a data architecture — but they're not trying to do the same thing.

ViceLab
Research & Instruments

ViceLab produces the evidence base that underpins every product in the ecosystem. Not academic research shelved in a journal — practical instruments, field guides, and education materials designed to be used by workers, not cited by researchers.

  • Peer-based methodology, not clinical

Cooked Pilot
Festival Safety Platform

A peer-to-peer safety app for festival-goers. Not a hotline. Not a government service. Cooked Pilot puts harm reduction in people's pockets — real-time substance alerts, check-in tools, and a Wingmate network that keeps groups accountable to each other.

  • Wingmate peer check-in system

Why trust this

What we stand on.

01
Peer-based, not prescriptive

Harm reduction works when it comes from people who understand the context. Our methodology is built around peer knowledge, not clinical hierarchy. We inform — we don't instruct.

02
Non-surveillance by design

Cooked Pilot and VibeGuard are built on a zero-surveillance commitment. No identifiable data is retained. No user behaviour is sold. The system works because people can trust it with their honesty.

03
Grounded in established practice

ViceLab's methodology aligns with organisations including Jellinek, The Loop, Energy Control, and Kosmicare — the field's established practitioners. We build on what already works.

04
Non-medical in scope

We are not a medical service and we don't claim to be. That boundary is intentional — it's what makes the system honest. We handle risk information; we refer for clinical response.

Our Position
Harm reduction is information. Not intervention.

"People will take drugs at festivals. That is not a problem to be solved — it's a reality to be navigated with care, accuracy, and respect for human autonomy."

ViceLab exists because the gap between what people do at festivals and what the infrastructure around them acknowledges is dangerous. Not dangerous because people are reckless — dangerous because the silence forces them to operate without information.

Every product in the ViceLab ecosystem is built on one principle: people make better decisions when they have better information. We don't moralize. We don't surveil. We don't pretend abstinence is a safety strategy.

We also don't pretend this is simple. Our ethics framework is available in full — because the people working with this system deserve to know what it was built on.
